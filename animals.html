Farm Animals

Horses

Horses are very beneficial on a farm. People can ride them to check on their herds, whether that be cattle, sheep,
or various other animals. Horses are also be used to drive cattle to pens so they can be doctored or sheep to the
barn to be sheared. They can also be ridden in many places where other vehicles cannot. This also makes them
extremely useful when navigating rough terrain. Draft horses have been used for many years to plow fields or
pull wagons, making them essential for farming also.

Beef Cows

There are many different breeds of beef cows. Some are Angus, Brahman, Hereford, and Charolais. Beef cattle
have been bred to produce muscle, not milk, which makes them much heftier than dairy cows.

Dairy Cows

There are also different breeds of dairy cows. Some are Holstein, Jersey, Brown Swiss, and Guernsey. Dairy
cows are leaner, not uncommon to see their hip bones, than beef cows. This is because they are bred to produce
milk.

Chickens

Chickens are also an essential farm animal. There are many different breeds of chickens, but they are separated
by meat chickens and laying hens. Meat chickens are raised specifically for their meat. Laying hens produce
eggs, which are collected daily.

Pigs

Pigs are another farm animal that is raised primarily for meat. Different breeds of pigs produce different quality
of meat. Some are better for bacon, while some are better for lard.

Goats

Goats are beneficial to farms because they can be used for multiple different reasons. They can just be pets and
keep your land clear by grazing, or they can be used for their milk, wool, or meat.

Sheep

Sheep are livestock who are raised for their wool. They are also raised for their milk and meat. Sheep milk
makes superior cheese and their meat is one of the healthier ones you can eat. Their wool has very durable
insulation and also regulates humidity.

Guardians

Livestock guardians can be different types of animals. The most common are donkeys and livestock guardian
dogs, or LGD. Livestock guardian dogs were specifically bred to protect livestock from potential predators.
Livestock guardians are around the herd they are protecting constantly. Donkeys tend to protect cattle, so they
stay in the same field as the cows at all times. LGD are aggressive towards predators, but very gentle with
people or small livestock.
